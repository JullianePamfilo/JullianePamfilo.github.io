Professional Self-Assessment

This is the “why me” section. I keep it readable, confident, and grounded.

Throughout my Computer Science program, I developed from a student learning foundational programming concepts into a confident and career-ready software engineer capable of designing, implementing, and improving real-world systems.

When I began the program, my focus was primarily on making programs function correctly. Over time, my perspective shifted toward building software that is scalable, efficient, maintainable, and secure. This transition is evident across my enhanced artifacts, where I revisited earlier work to apply stronger architectural decisions, optimized data structures, and improved validation and error handling.

Together, these artifacts demonstrate that I can design and evaluate computing solutions, apply appropriate algorithms and data structures, and manage data responsibly. Beyond the code, I value documentation and clarity because strong engineering depends on communication as much as implementation.
